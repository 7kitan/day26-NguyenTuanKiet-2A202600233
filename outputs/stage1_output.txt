Question: What are the employer's legal obligations when terminating an employee under Vietnamese labor law?
----------------------------------------------------------------------

>>> Calling LLM directly (no tools, no RAG)...

Under Vietnamese labor law, employers have several legal obligations when terminating an employee:

1. **Valid Grounds**: Termination must be based on valid grounds outlined in the Labor Code, such as repeated failure to perform duties, prolonged illness, or organizational restructuring.

2. **Notice Period**: Employers must provide advance written notice—30 days for indefinite-term contracts and 3 days for definite-term contracts—unless termination is due to serious misconduct.

3. **Severance Pay**: Employees are entitled to severance pay if they have worked for at least 12 months. The amount is half a month’s salary for each year of employment.

4. **Unused Leave**: Employers must compensate for unused annual leave.

5. **Social Insurance**: Employers must complete social insurance procedures and ensure employees receive any entitled benefits.

6. **Trade Union Consultation**: For unionized employees, employers must consult with the trade union before termination.

7. **Written Notice**: Termination must be documented in writing, specifying the reason and effective date.

Failure to comply with these obligations may result in legal disputes or penalties. Employers should ensure adherence to all procedural and substantive requirements under the Labor Code.
