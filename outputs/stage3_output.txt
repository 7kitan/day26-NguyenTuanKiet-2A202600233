STAGE 3: Single Agent (ReAct Loop)
======================================================================

[How it works]
  1. An autonomous agent receives a complex multi-part question
  2. It reasons about what tools to call (Think)
  3. It calls a tool (Act)
  4. It observes the result and decides next steps (Observe)
  5. It repeats until it has enough information for a final answer

### 1. **Contract Breach Consequences**
   - **Penalties**: Estimated base penalty of $250,000, plus consequential damages, attorney's fees, and potential injunctions.
   - **Legal Actions**: Users may file lawsuits for breach of contract, seeking compensation for damages incurred due to the violation.

### 2. **Data Privacy Violations**
   - **CCPA**: Fines up to $7,500 per intentional violation. Users also have the right to sue for $100–$750 per consumer in case of data breaches.
   - **GDPR**: If EU users are affected, fines can reach up to 4% of global revenue or EUR 20 million.
   - **FTC Act Section 5**: Penalties for unfair or deceptive practices, including sharing user data without consent.
   - **Class Action Lawsuits**: Users may file class action lawsuits under state privacy laws.

### 3. **Compliance Requirements**
   - **Applicable Frameworks**: CCPA/CPRA, GDPR (if EU users are involved), FTC Act Section 5, and SOC 2.

### Summary
The startup faces significant financial penalties, potential lawsuits, and reputational damage. Immediate steps should include:
1. Addressing the contract breach and compensating affected users.
2. Ensuring compliance with CCPA, GDPR, and FTC regulations.
3. Implementing SOC 2 Type II certification to strengthen data security practices.

This analysis highlights the legal risks and compliance obligations the startup must address to mitigate further consequences.
